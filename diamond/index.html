history

The word "rhombus" comes from Ancient Greek: ῥόμβος, romanized: rhombos, meaning something that spins,[3] which derives from the verb ῥέμβω, romanized: rhémbō, meaning "to turn round and round."[4] The word was used both by Euclid and Archimedes, who used the term "solid rhombus" for a bicone, two right circular cones sharing a common base
