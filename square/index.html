square (noun)

ˈskwer

a : a rectangle with all four sides equal

b : the product of a number multiplied by itself

c : something resembling a heart in shape

history

In Euclidean geometry, a square is a regular quadrilateral, which means that it has four equal sides and four equal angles (90-degree angles, π/2 radian angles, or right angles). It can also be defined as a rectangle with two equal-length adjacent sides. It is the only regular polygon whose internal angle, central angle, and external angle are all equal (90°), and whose diagonals are all equal in length.
