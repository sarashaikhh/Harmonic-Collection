triangle (noun)

tri·​an·​gle

a :  a polygon having three sides

b : a percussion instrument consisting of a rod of steel bent into the form of a triangle open at one angle and sounded by striking with a small metal rod

c : a situation in which one member of a couple is involved in a love affair with a third person

history

The triangle is one of the earliest known geometric shapes, with evidence of its use dating back to ancient Mesopotamia (modern-day Iraq), over 5000 years ago. Its name was derived from the Latin word ”triangulus,” meaning “three-cornered”.

Triangles can be used in art in many different ways. They can be used to make optical illusions, to add details to many different things and can even be used to make things like teeth, spikes, swords, or blades of grass. Triangles are used to bring harmony to the artists drawing, sketch or painting.
